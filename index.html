Introduction

Our work will revolve around the extensively studied and impressively rich Global Terrorism Database. The GTD includes data on over 200,000 international terrorist attacks since 1970 and has information on over 100 features for each terrorist attack. We direct our analysis at the differences between terrorist groups with different ideologies. We start by providing a geographical overview of terrorist attacks. Afterwards, we consider how the ideological background of terrorists has evolved over time. Then, we dive into how terrorist groups from different ideologies differ.

Mapping terrorism: where hate strikes.

Let us start with our geographical overview of terrorist attacks. Below, you can see an interactive map, made through Kepler.gl. This map contains arcs connecting the country of origin of each terrorist group to the location of their attacks. The blue end of the arc indicates the country of origin of the terrorist group, while the red end of the arc indicates the location of the attack. What can you discover in the graph?

Divided We Stand: Unpacking Terrorist Group Disparities
