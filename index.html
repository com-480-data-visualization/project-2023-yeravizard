CLUSTER BOMB

Uncovering Patterns in Terrorist Groups

This website consists of three parts. First, we present a general analysis of terrorism over time. Second, we discuss the differences between terrorist attacks that are executed by groups with different ideologies. Last, we zoom in on the terrorist groups themselves and discuss the differences between groups of different ideologies.

Our work will revolve around the extensively studied and impressively rich Global Terrorism Database. The GTD includes data on over 200,000 international terrorist attacks since 1970 and has information on over 100 features for each terrorist attack. We direct our analysis at the differences between terrorist groups with different ideologies.

Mapping terrorism: where hate strikes.

Our main visualization in this part is an interactive map of terrorist attacks around the world, made using Kepler.gl. This map contains arcs connecting the country of origin of each terrorist group to the location of their attacks.

Divided We Stand: Unpacking Terrorist Group Disparities

In this section, we will dive deeper into what makes a terrorist group. Specifically, we will consider three different groups, with three different ideologies. By comparing these groups, we will gain a deeper understanding about the key features of of a terrorist group.
